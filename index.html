February 2018

This month, I was selected for my class to represent Information Technology and the school to take part in the New York City SKILLS USA Competition. The school I attended was the School of Cooperative Technical Education. I loved that school. I was going there while going through my first year of college at the same time. It was hard but I managed my time and was able to achieve my goals. I learned a lot about Information Technology. Building computers, troubleshooting, setting up new hardware, documentating/fixing hardware and software issues. The SKILLS USA Competition gave me the opportunity to not only compete but learn and interact with others. I had fun learning from my peers there. I felt alot of pressure since it was the first time I ever competed against other students. The tasks during the competition was first build a computer and successfully get it to the BIOS setup. The second task is to assemble a functional Ethernet cable. Test it using a Network Cable Tester to make sure it works. The final task was to troubleshoot some software issues and change minor details for the user on a desktop. I came in 2nd Place. I was still really proud of myself to have come in 2nd place. I never thought I would be a part of something that important and huge in the first place. I was happy and had a ton of fun. I wasn't expecting to recieve a medal but did and I will always remember the time I not only felt pressure but still practiced and worked hard to have come in 2nd Place.
